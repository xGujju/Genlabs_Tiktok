### Durable patterns for GenLabs
1. Hook = unanswered question. The first slide should create a concrete curiosity gap that a broad Thai viewer cannot resolve without swiping: “ทำไมสิ่งนี้ถึงสำคัญกับฉันตอนนี้?”
2. Use “question planting” across slides, not only on slide 1. Every slide should answer one question and open the next one.
3. Avoid first-impression mismatch. Slide 1 promise, visual, caption, and final takeaway must match. Do not bait with “AI ใหม่แรงมาก” if the carousel is actually a narrow technical release.
4. Compress speed-to-value. Put a usable takeaway by slide 2; do not spend early slides on background unless the background itself is shocking.
5. Use staccato Thai copy: short lines, high contrast, one idea per line. This fits handwritten carousel style and reduces cognitive load.
6. Treat TikTok slideshows like a retention object: optimize 2-second hold, 6-second hold, swipes, saves, shares, and follows. For each post, record whether the first slide likely earned a pause and whether the final slide earned a save/follow.
7. Mine TikTok Creative Center for “most valuable frame” thinking. For GenLabs, define our “most valuable frame” as the slide most likely to trigger save/share/follow, not just visual prettiness.
8. Carousel content should encourage interaction and sharing by packaging identity-useful advice: mistakes to avoid, quick checklists, before/after workflows, templates, and “send this to a friend who uses AI.”
9. Algorithm guidance is less useful than behavior guidance: relevance/content information, engagement, user interactions, and search cues should translate into simple Thai keywords in on-slide text and caption.
10. For AI education, broad mass hooks beat insider hooks. Convert model/tool releases into everyday Thai stakes: money, work speed, privacy, school/work documents, scams, mistakes, and prompts people can copy.

### GenLabs experiments to run
- Experiment: “Question Ladder Carousel” — 5 slides where each slide title is a question. Measure completion proxy via saves/shares/follows against a normal explainer.
- Experiment: “Value by Slide 2” — slide 1 hook, slide 2 copyable prompt/checklist, slides 3-5 context. Compare against background-first AI news posts.
- Experiment: “Most Valuable Frame QA” — before publishing, tag one slide as MVF: save/share/follow trigger. If no slide earns that label, rewrite before posting.
